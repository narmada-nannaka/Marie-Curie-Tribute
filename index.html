Timeline

1867
Born in Warsaw, Poland.
1891
Moved to Paris to continue her studies.
1895
Married to Pierre Curie.
1897
Gave birth to Irene.
1903
Appointed as the Head of the Physics Laboratory at the Sarbonne and received her Doctor of Science degree.
1904
Gave Birth to Eve.
1906
Noted as the first woman to hold the position of Professor of General Physics in the Faculty of Sciences.
1908
Became the titular professor.
1911
Became a member of the Conseil du Physique Solvay.
1922
Became a member of the Committee of Intellectual Co-operation of the League of Nations.

Awards

1903
Won Noble Prize in physics along with her husband, Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel. Also, noted as the first woman to win a Noble Prize in physics.
1910
Fundamental treatise on radioactivity is published.
1911
Awarded Noble Prize in Chemistry for discovery radium and polonium elements.
